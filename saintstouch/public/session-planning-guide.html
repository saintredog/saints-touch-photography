02
Wardrobe

What to Wear

Your wardrobe sets the emotional tone of the entire session. You don't need to be formal — you need to feel like yourself on your best day. Here's how to approach it:

Wardrobe that tells a story

Bring 2–3 outfit options. We'll pick the best one together on the day. Different looks give us variety and create more usable images from a single session.

🎨
Stick to a palette
Neutrals, earth tones, and muted colors photograph beautifully. Cream, olive, burgundy, navy — all work. Avoid neon or heavy patterns.
🧵
Texture wins
Linen, silk, velvet, knit — these add depth and dimension on camera. Flat, shiny synthetics often look cheap in photos even if they don't in person.
📐
Fit matters most
Well-fitted clothes photograph a thousand times better than anything baggy or ill-fitting. When in doubt, go slightly more tailored.
👟
Don't forget shoes
Shoes appear in more shots than you'd think. Match or coordinate with your look — worn-out sneakers with a dress can break the whole image.

    Do Wear

  • Solid colors and subtle patterns
  • Layers you can remove or add
  • Clothing you feel confident in
  • Well-fitting silhouettes
  • Coordinated (not matching) for couples/groups

    Avoid

  • Logos or large text
  • Wrinkled clothing (iron beforehand)
  • Overly busy prints
  • Anything that doesn't fit you now
  • Head-to-toe identical outfits in groups

03
Preparation

Hair & Beauty

You don't need to look dramatically different from how you normally look. In fact, the best results come from an elevated version of your everyday self — not a costume.

💄
Makeup
Slightly more than your everyday look. Camera tends to soften features — a bit more definition goes a long way. Matte finishes photograph better than shine.
💇
Hair
Schedule any haircut 1–2 weeks before (not the day before). Bring styling products. Have a backup plan — a simple updo or natural look in case humidity acts up.
💅
Nails
Your hands will appear in many shots. Clean, neutral nails or a fresh manicure makes a real difference. Skip the chipped polish.
🌿
Skin
Moisturize the day before. If you're prone to redness, bring blotting papers. Avoid drastic tanning or skin treatments within a week of the session.

"The goal isn't perfection — it's presence. When you feel good, it shows."

04
Location & Light

Timing Your Session

Light is everything in photography. The difference between a stunning image and an average one is often just when the shot was taken, not where.

🌅
Golden Hour
The hour after sunrise and hour before sunset create soft, warm, directional light that's flattering for portraits. This is when outdoor sessions shine.
☁️
Overcast Days
Don't cancel if it's cloudy. Overcast skies act as a giant soft box — even, flattering light with no harsh shadows. Some of the best portraits happen on grey days.
🏙️
Urban Locations
Brick walls, alleyways, rooftops, parking decks — architecture creates incredible backdrops and geometric interest. Perfect for editorial and personal brand work.
🌳
Nature Settings
Fields, forests, coastlines — natural settings bring a timeless quality. Virginia Beach has incredible variety within 30 minutes.

Not sure on a location? Tell me your vibe and I'll suggest 2–3 options. I know the best spots around Virginia Beach and the Hampton Roads area — including the ones that aren't overcrowded.

05
Checklist

What to Bring

Pack a bag the night before. These are the essentials:

  • 2–3 outfits — steamed and ready to wear
  • Comfortable shoes for walking between locations
  • Touch-up kit — blotting papers, lipstick/gloss, small mirror
  • Hair products/backup plan — travel-size spray, pins, hair tie
  • Water bottle — sessions can run 1–3 hours; stay hydrated
  • Any meaningful props — instruments, books, heirlooms, florals
  • A light snack — especially for early-morning golden hour sessions
  • Reference images — if you have a Pinterest board or specific poses in mind, bring it
  • Open mind — some of the best shots come from moments we didn't plan
06
Day of Your Session

What to Expect

Here's what a typical session flow looks like — from arrival to wrap:

Arrive

15 minutes early

Gives you time to settle, get comfortable, and do any last-minute touch-ups. Rushing straight into shooting creates tension that shows up in photos.

First 15

Warm-up shots

We'll talk, scout the spot, and ease in. These frames aren't meant to be your hero shots — they're for calibrating light, angles, and getting you relaxed. Don't worry how these look.

Mid

The flow state

This is when it clicks. You've forgotten about the camera, you're moving naturally, and I'm catching the real moments. Trust the process — this is where your best images come from.

Outfit ②

Quick change

If you brought multiple outfits, we'll swap here. We may move locations too. Keep the momentum going — don't overthink the second look.

Wrap

Final frames

We'll end with a few intentional, specific shots — any particular poses or setups you wanted. Then we review a few images together before calling it.

07
Direction

Posing & Direction

Directed movement, authentic result

You don't need to know how to pose. That's my job. I'll direct you through movements — not static positions — because movement creates natural, alive expressions far better than "hold still and smile."

Expect to walk, turn, look away and back, laugh at something I say (or don't say), and forget you're being photographed. That's the goal.

🚶
Move naturally
Walk toward me, away from me, turn slowly. Movement creates flow in images that static posing never can.
😊
Don't force the smile
A real smile takes half a second to arrive after something genuinely amusing. I'll work to create those moments — your job is to let them happen.
👁️
Eyes are everything
Relax your eyes — don't over-widen them. Slightly squinting (called "smizing") creates warmth and avoids the deer-in-headlights look.
🗣️
Speak up
If something feels awkward or you want to try something specific, say so. This is your session. I want you to love every image.
